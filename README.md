This is a business consulting pitch presentation created by Kinetic Consulting (a University of Denver student team) for the Tenaska Business Challenge, delivered on February 23, 2024. Here's a summary:
The Problem: The University of Aksarben is constructing new buildings that will increase energy demand by 25%, and needs a plan to meet this demand while aligning with a 15-year sustainability vision.
The Proposed Solution — "ERA" (a three-phase energy strategy):
The team evaluated three options — plant expansion, utility grid supply, and PV solar panels — using a weighted decision matrix. Solar panels scored highest and became the core recommendation, branded as the "ERA" plan:

Phase 1 (Years 1–6): Land acquisition, permitting, and a 6-year utility grid supply contract as a reliable bridge (~$17.2M in costs)
Phase 2 (Years 4–6): Installation of 12 MW of PV solar panels and energy storage
Phase 3 (Year 15): Installation of the remaining 13 MW of solar, completing the full buildout

Financials: Total costs run approximately $59M in years 1–6 and $40M in years 7–15, compared to a much costlier plant expansion alternative.
Marketing Strategy — "Triple A":
A parallel marketing campaign targeting three audiences to build support and offset costs:

Alumni — annual fundraising banquets
Athletes — stadium solar integration, sponsorships, and awareness booths
Aspiring Students — tuition mitigation, scholarships, course expansion, and reliability communication to parents

The presentation closes with a mission statement — "Sustain the university to sustain the future" — and includes an appendix with detailed financial breakdowns, assumptions, risk analysis, and sources.
